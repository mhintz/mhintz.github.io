Net Art:

What's Cool About Art on the Net?

Here's What's Cool:

- Emphasize that the work is often inextricably part of the browser
- Mention how revolutionary internet distribution is to the art world
- Mention that the art often questions how we use the internet, and what the internet is doing to our attitudes, social lives, senses of self, etc.
- Emphasize that the web as a platform is being developed and expanded every day, so the medium rapidly changes and improves. This is not a feature you get with, say, painting.

Olia Lialina - Summer

  • (link to view-source)
The piece is playful, even carefree, in the way that we often use the internet - hopping between different sites casually, like someone swinging.

Young-Hae Chang Heavy Industries Present - DAKOTA

- Emphasize that the piece is pretty much a video
- There's no interaction, yet it clears away everything except the most essential thing.
- It isn't a sharing platform or meant to be stopped and started. It's meant to be played all the way through, which is the experience of the art.
- Note that many net art works exist, like this one, as a standalone web page that is entirely the art, and nothing more. (Not like, for instance, the way a flash game is presented).

Yung Jake - e.m-bed.de/d/

- One of my favorites.
- Filled with jokes and statements about sites on the internet, the way we use the internet, and the way using the internet changes how we interact with each other.
- Even the title is a sly pun about how, if his video is shared and embedded on other pages (or laid out, as in HTML), he'll get laid ("embedded").

Vince Mckelvie - Honey

- Another music video example. Interactive net art exists too!
- In fact, Vince Mckelvie was involved in a couple such projects - Club Rothko Builder and klear.me

Banger Briz - Three.js Playground

- Art that's interactive, participatory, shareable - like the internet!
- The creator is much less involved in the art here, preferring to create a space for users to generate their own creations

Rafael Rozendaal - if no yes . com

- Another interactive piece, with a distinct, minimal aesthetic.
- Notable for having recently been sold at auction.

Net Art:

Why is Net Art Interesting?

Here's Why:

- Internet is an open platform, and the source code for most Net Art is usually unobfuscated.
- It's possible to visit a net art site, copy all the code, and reuse at will.
- Users aren't expressly licensed to do so, but taking and remixing is part of Net Art culture (see Banger Briz piece, above)

Net Art As Challenge to Art Establishment

- Free distribution challenges traditional models of gallery viewing and private collection
- Difficult for the big art institutions to work with net art, except by putting computers in the galleries.

Net Art As Creative Use of The Internet

- There's a pretty large creative coding community, including a sizeable chunk focused on the internet
- Mention flash
- Mention the incomplete overlap between net artists and creative coders
- some net art is produced on a contract basis (e.g. Rafael Rozendaal)
- You also see unofficial groups ("movements"?), like the tumblr community of people who make interesting gifs with Processing
- Mention the liberating feeling of building an app for purely visual, creative purposes

Paddles ON! Auction

- How odd is it that private art collectors have purchased gifs and html?
- I expect a lot more of this kind of traditional establishment-net art crossover in the future.
- Net Art is one of the hot areas of current contemporary art.

How Do You Make Net Art?

2D Libraries

  • EaselJS
  • SVG.js
  • Two.js
  • Raphael.js
  • Paper.js
- Many different 2D graphics libraries
- Some use canvas, some use SVG, some are renderer-agnostic
- Good for data visualization! (3D, not so much. Prone to exploding 3D pie charts)

3D with THREE.js

- The Three.js API is still in Beta, and it changes frequently
- A familiar problem in open source: the documentation doesn't nearly keep up with development
- Understanding of fundamental 3D graphics concepts is often assumed, even in tutorials
- Tutorials sometimes just don't work with recent revision version
